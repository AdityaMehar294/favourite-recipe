Aloo Paratha

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • 3-4 medium-sized potatoes (boiled and mashed)
  • 1 small onion (finely chopped)
  • 1-2 green chilies (finely chopped)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • Fresh coriander leaves (finely chopped)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Ghee or oil (for cooking)

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the whole wheat flour and a pinch of salt. Gradually add water and knead the mixture into a smooth and soft dough. Cover it and let it rest for at least 15-20 minutes.
  2. For the Potato Filling:
    • In a mixing bowl, combine the mashed potatoes, chopped onion, green chilies, ginger-garlic paste, cumin seeds, turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, chopped coriander leaves, and salt. Mix well.
    • Divide the potato mixture into small, lemon-sized balls. Set aside.
  3. Assembling and Cooking:
    1. Take a portion of the dough and roll it into a small ball. Flatten it with your palms.
    2. Roll the flattened dough ball into a small disc.
    3. Place one of the potato balls in the center of the disc.
    4. Bring the edges of the disc together, covering the potato mixture completely. Seal the edges to form a stuffed ball.
    5. Gently flatten the stuffed ball and roll it out into a paratha, ensuring the filling is evenly distributed.
    6. Heat a griddle or a tawa over medium heat. Place the rolled paratha on the hot surface.
    7. Cook for a minute or so, then flip the paratha and apply ghee or oil on the cooked side.
    8. Flip again and apply ghee or oil on the other side. Cook until both sides are golden brown and crisp.
    9. Repeat the process for the remaining dough and potato filling.
  4. Serve the Aloo Parathas hot with yogurt, pickles, or your favorite chutney. Enjoy your delicious homemade Aloo Parathas!

Aloo Gobhi Matar

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cauliflower florets
  • 1 cup green peas (fresh or frozen)
  • 2 medium-sized potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, chopped (adjust according to your spice preference)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 3 tablespoons oil
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Vegetables:
    1. Cut the cauliflower into florets.
    2. Peel and cube the potatoes.
    3. If using fresh peas, shell them; if using frozen peas, thaw them.
  2. Blanch Cauliflower:
    1. Boil water in a pot, add a pinch of salt.
    2. Add cauliflower florets and peas to the boiling water for about 3-4 minutes.
    3. Drain and keep aside.
  3. Cooking the Vegetables:
    1. Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
    2. Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
    3. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
    4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and the oil starts to separate.
  4. Add Spices:
    1. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for a couple of minutes.
  5. Add Vegetables:
    1. Add the blanched cauliflower florets, peas, and cubed potatoes to the pan. Mix gently to coat the vegetables with the spice mixture.
  6. Cover and Cook:
    1. Cover the pan with a lid and cook on low to medium heat. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.
    2. Cook until the vegetables are tender but not mushy. It usually takes around 15-20 minutes.
  7. Finish with Garam Masala:
    1. Once the vegetables are cooked, add garam masala and stir well.
  8. Garnish and Serve:
    1. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
    2. Serve hot with roti, naan, or rice.

cake

Ingredients:

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup (2 sticks) unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1 1/4 cups whole milk

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt. Set aside.
  3. In a large mixing bowl, cream together the softened butter and sugar until light and fluffy.
  4. Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Stir in the vanilla extract.
  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, alternating with the milk. Begin and end with the dry ingredients.
  6. Divide the batter evenly between the prepared cake pans and smooth the tops with a spatula.
  7. Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  8. Remove the cakes from the oven and let them cool in the pans for 10 minutes. Then, transfer the cakes to a wire rack to cool completely.
  9. Once the cakes are completely cool, you can frost and decorate them as desired.

Dal Makhani

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup whole black gram (urad dal)
  • 1/4 cup kidney beans (rajma)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Rinse and soak the black gram (urad dal) and kidney beans (rajma) in water overnight.
  2. Cook the soaked dal and rajma in a pressure cooker until soft and well-cooked.
  3. In a separate pan, heat butter and add cumin seeds. Sauté until they splutter.
  4. Add finely chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  5. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
  6. Add pureed tomatoes, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Cook until the oil starts to separate.
  7. Add the cooked dal and rajma to the tomato mixture. Mix well and let it simmer on low heat.
  8. Add garam masala and cream. Stir until well combined.
  9. Simmer the dal makhani for an additional 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
  10. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves before serving.
  11. Serve hot with naan or rice.

Chana Masala

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked chickpeas (canned or boiled)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  2. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Sauté for a minute.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and the oil separates.
  5. Add coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for a couple of minutes.
  6. Add cooked chickpeas and garam masala. Mix to coat chickpeas with the spice mixture.
  7. Add water if needed and simmer for 10-15 minutes until the flavors meld.
  8. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  9. Serve hot with rice, naan, or chapati.

Coriander Chutney

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh coriander leaves, washed and chopped
  • 1/2 cup fresh mint leaves, washed and chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, chopped (adjust according to your spice preference)
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon grated coconut (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon roasted chana dal (split gram)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, chopped
  • 1-2 cloves of garlic (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Water as needed

Instructions:

  1. In a blender or food processor, combine fresh coriander leaves, mint leaves, green chilies, chopped onion, grated coconut (if using), roasted chana dal, cumin seeds, ginger, and garlic (if using).
  2. Add salt to taste and lemon juice to the mixture.
  3. Blend the ingredients until you get a smooth paste. Add water gradually to achieve your desired consistency.
  4. Taste and adjust salt and lemon juice as needed.
  5. Transfer the chutney to a serving bowl.
  6. You can temper the chutney with mustard seeds, urad dal, and curry leaves for added flavor (optional).
  7. Serve the coriander chutney with dosa, idli, samosa, or any other snack of your choice.

Saag Paneer

Ingredients:

  • 250g paneer, cubed
  • 500g fresh spinach, washed and chopped
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
    • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
    • 2 green chilies, chopped
    • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
    • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
    • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
    • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
    • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
    • Salt to taste
    • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
    • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Blanch Spinach:
    • Boil a pot of water, add a pinch of salt.
    • Add chopped spinach to boiling water for about 2-3 minutes.
    • Drain and transfer the spinach to ice-cold water.
    • Blend the blanched spinach into a smooth puree.
  2. Prepare Paneer:
    • Heat 1 tablespoon of oil or ghee in a pan.
    • Fry the paneer cubes until golden brown. Set aside.
  3. Cooking the Base:
    • In the same pan, add 1 tablespoon of oil or ghee.
    • Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
    • Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
    • Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Sauté for a minute.

Saffron Rice

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 2 cups water
  • A pinch of saffron strands
  • 2 tablespoons warm milk
  • 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced
  • 2-3 green cardamom pods
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear.
  2. Soak the saffron strands in warm milk and set aside.
  3. In a pot, heat ghee over medium heat.
  4. Add sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
  5. Add cardamom pods and cinnamon stick to the onions, sauté for an additional minute.
  6. Add the rinsed basmati rice to the pot and stir to coat the rice with the ghee and spices.
  7. Pour in the water, add saffron-soaked milk, and season with salt.
  8. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer until the rice is cooked and the liquid is absorbed (about 15-20 minutes).
  9. Once cooked, fluff the rice with a fork.
  10. Garnish with chopped fresh coriander before serving.
